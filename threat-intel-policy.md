2. Purpose
This policy establishes the requirements for the collection, analysis, dissemination, and operational use of threat intelligence to:
•	Protect enterprise assets and data
•	Enable proactive threat detection and response
•	Support risk-informed decision-making
________________________________________
3. Scope
3.1 In Scope
•	All business units, subsidiaries, and geographies
•	All IT, cloud, and OT environments
•	Security operations, incident response, and threat hunting
3.2 Out of Scope
•	Non-cyber intelligence domains unless approved
________________________________________
4. Policy Statement
The organization shall implement a formal Threat Intelligence Program that:
•	Is integrated with Security Operations (SOC)
•	Follows a defined intelligence lifecycle
•	Uses validated and trusted intelligence sources
•	Supports automation and scalability
•	Aligns with enterprise risk management
________________________________________
5. Governance Structure
5.1 Oversight
•	The CISO is accountable for the Threat Intelligence Program
•	Oversight provided by:
o	Cyber Risk Committee
o	Security Governance Board
5.2 Operating Functions
•	Threat Intelligence Team
•	Security Operations Center (SOC)
•	Incident Response Team
________________________________________
6. Threat Intelligence Lifecycle
The organization shall implement the following lifecycle:
1.	Direction (define intelligence requirements)
2.	Collection (gather data)
3.	Processing (normalize, deduplicate)
4.	Analysis (contextualize threats)
5.	Dissemination (share intelligence)
6.	Feedback (continuous improvement)
________________________________________
7. Intelligence Requirements
7.1 Priority Intelligence Requirements (PIRs)
Must be defined and reviewed quarterly:
•	Threats targeting industry sector
•	Cloud and infrastructure threats
•	Ransomware and advanced persistent threats
________________________________________
8. Intelligence Sources
8.1 Approved Sources
•	Internal telemetry (logs, EDR, incidents)
•	Open-source intelligence (OSINT)
•	Commercial intelligence providers
•	Industry sharing groups (ISACs)
8.2 Source Validation
•	All sources must be evaluated for:
o	Accuracy
o	Reliability
o	Timeliness
________________________________________
9. Data Handling & Classification
9.1 Classification
Threat intelligence shall be classified as:
•	Public
•	Internal
•	Confidential
•	Restricted
9.2 Data Protection
•	Encryption at rest and in transit
•	Role-based access control (RBAC)
•	Logging and monitoring of access
________________________________________
10. Intelligence Analysis
10.1 Requirements
•	Intelligence must be:
o	Actionable
o	Relevant
o	Timely
10.2 Framework Alignment
•	Analysis shall align with MITRE ATT&CK for TTP mapping
________________________________________
11. Integration with Security Operations
11.1 SOC Integration
•	Intelligence must be integrated with SIEM and SOAR
•	Used for alert enrichment and prioritization
11.2 Incident Response
•	Intelligence must support:
o	Triage
o	Investigation
o	Containment
11.3 Threat Hunting
•	Intelligence shall drive proactive threat hunting
________________________________________
12. Automation & Technology
The organization shall:
•	Implement a Threat Intelligence Platform (TIP)
•	Integrate with:
o	SIEM
o	SOAR
o	EDR/XDR
•	Automate:
o	IOC ingestion
o	Alert enrichment
o	Response actions
________________________________________
13. Threat Intelligence Sharing
13.1 Internal Sharing
•	SOC dashboards
•	Intelligence reports
•	Executive briefings
13.2 External Sharing
•	Only through approved channels:
o	ISACs
o	Government agencies
o	Trusted partners
13.3 Restrictions
•	Sensitive intelligence must not be shared externally without approval
________________________________________
14. Compliance & Legal
The program shall comply with:
•	Data privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR)
•	Industry standards (ISO 27001, NIST CSF)
•	Legal restrictions on intelligence sharing
________________________________________
15. Metrics & Reporting
15.1 Key Metrics
•	Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)
•	Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)
•	% of alerts enriched by intelligence
•	Threat coverage (MITRE ATT&CK)
15.2 Reporting
•	Daily operational reports
•	Weekly intelligence summaries
•	Monthly executive reports
________________________________________
16. Risk Management
16.1 Risks
•	Inaccurate intelligence
•	Data overload
•	False positives
16.2 Mitigation
•	Source validation
•	Confidence scoring
•	Continuous monitoring
________________________________________
17. Roles & Responsibilities
Role	Responsibility
CISO	Program ownership
TI Analysts	Intelligence analysis
SOC Analysts	Operational use
Engineers	Integration & automation
Risk Team	Oversight
________________________________________
18. Training & Awareness
•	All SOC and TI personnel must receive:
o	Threat intelligence training
o	Tool-specific training
•	Annual refresher required
________________________________________
19. Exceptions
•	Exceptions must be:
o	Documented
o	Approved by the CISO
o	Reviewed periodically
________________________________________
20. Enforcement
•	Non-compliance may result in:
o	Disciplinary action
o	Access revocation
o	Escalation to leadership
________________________________________
21. Review & Maintenance
•	Policy reviewed annually
•	Updated based on:
o	Threat landscape changes
o	Technology evolution
o	Regulatory updates

and enables a shift toward:
Proactive and intelligence-driven cybersecurity operations
