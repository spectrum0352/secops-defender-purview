o HUMINT - Human: It is an intelligence type obtained from a person in the field.
o GEOINT - Geographical: It is an intelligence type obtained using satellite and aerial photographs.
o SIGINT - Signal: It is a type of intelligence obtained by interfering with signals.

Lifecycle

The cyber threat intelligence lifecycle is a continuous process. Organizations should regularly review and update their threat intelligence program to ensure that it is meeting their needs.

The cyber threat intelligence lifecycle is a process of gathering, analysing, and disseminating threat intelligence to support cybersecurity operations. It is a continuous process that involves the following steps:

• Planning: Determining the purpose, objective and requirements of the CTI
• Collection: Collecting data from many sources
• Processing: Processing the collected data and making it ready for analysis
• Analysis: Analyzing the processed data, transforming the information into intelligence and making it ready for sharing
• Dissemination: Sharing threat intelligence data
• Feedback: Determining whether arrangements should be made for future threat intelligence operations by taking feedback from the reports shared.
• Requirements: The first step is to identify the requirements for the threat intelligence program. This includes understanding the organization's assets, risks, and threats. The requirements will also specify the type of threat intelligence that is needed and how it will be used.
• Collection: Once the requirements are defined, the next step is to collect threat intelligence. This can be done from a variety of sources, including open-source intelligence (OSINT), closed-source intelligence (CSINT), and threat sharing communities.
• Processing: The collected threat intelligence needs to be processed to make it useful for analysis. This may involve cleaning the data, normalizing it, and converting it into a format that can be easily analysed.
• Analysis: The processed threat intelligence is then analysed to identify patterns, trends, and insights. This analysis can be used to identify new threats, develop threat detection rules, and improve incident response procedures.
• Dissemination: The analysed threat intelligence is then disseminated to the appropriate stakeholders. This may include security analysts, incident responders, and risk managers. The threat intelligence should be disseminated in a format that is easy to understand and actionable.
• Feedback: The final step in the cyber threat intelligence lifecycle is feedback. This involves collecting feedback from the stakeholders who received the threat intelligence to determine whether it was useful and actionable. The feedback can then be used to improve the threat intelligence program.

Threat Indicator Metadata
• Threat Indicator Metadata
• Timestamp: When the CTI was collected
• Risk score: relative maliciousness of the CTI
• Source: the origin of the CTI
• Geolocation: the physical location of the host that presents the threat
• Threat category: anonymous proxy, bogon, bot, botnet, malware, passive DNS, etc.
• Note: Not all metadata is equally important

Cyber Threat Intelligence Types
• There are 3 types of cyber threat intelligence.
• Tactical Cyber Threat Intelligence: Tactical CTI provides more specific details about the tactics, techniques and procedures of threat actors, also known as TTP. Usually Tactical CTI reports contain IOCs (data such as IP address, hash).  The following people or products usually use the type of strategic cyber threat intelligence.
o SOC Analyst
o Security Products (IPS/IDS/EDR/Firewall)
o SIEM Products
• Operational Cyber Threat Intelligence: It allows you to understand cyber threat actor's motivations, goals and TTPs.
o Threat Hunters
o Incident Responders
o Security Engineers
• Strategic Cyber Threat Intelligence: It is an intelligence type that provides detailed analysis of trends and emerging risks to create an overall picture of the possible consequences of a cyber-attack.
o C Level Executives
o Managers

Threat Intelligence Detection Best Practices
• Predict and Prioritize Security Weakness
o Gather threat intelligence information
o Manage vulnerabilities and risk
o Augment vulnerability scan data with context for optimized prioritization
o Manage device configurations (Firewalls, switches, routers, IPS/IDS)
• Detect deviations to identify malicious activity
o Establish baseline behaviors
o Monitor and investigate anomalies
o Monitor network flows
• React in real time to exploits
o Correlate logs, events, network flows, identities, assets, vulnerabilities and configurations and add context.
o Use automated and cognitive solutions to make data accessible by existing staff.
