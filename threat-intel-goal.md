Goals of Threat intelligence

Goals
• Increase awareness and understanding of cyber threats. CTI can help organizations understand the latest threats, the threat actors behind them, and their tactics and techniques. This information can be used to inform security decisions and reduce the risk of cyberattacks.
• Improve the ability to detect and respond to cyber threats. CTI can help organizations develop new threat detection rules, improve their incident response procedures, and make better decisions about how to allocate security resources.
• Support risk assessment and security planning. CTI can help organizations identify and assess their cyber risks and develop security plans to mitigate those risks.
• Facilitate collaboration and information sharing among organizations. CTI can help organizations share information about cyber threats with each other, which can help everyone improve their security posture.
• Identifying new and emerging threats
• Tracking the activity of known threat actors
• Understanding the motivations and capabilities of threat actors
• Developing threat detection rules and incident response procedures
• Assessing the risk of cyber threats
• Developing security plans to mitigate cyber risks
• Sharing information about cyber threats with other organizations

Here are some specific examples of how CTI can be used to achieve these goals:
• A financial services company can use CTI to identify new phishing campaigns targeting its employees. This information can then be used to develop new training programs for employees and to implement new security controls.
• A healthcare organization can use CTI to track the activity of known ransomware gangs. This information can then be used to develop new detection and response procedures for ransomware attacks.
• A government agency can use CTI to understand the motivations and capabilities of nation-state actors. This information can then be used to assess the risk of being targeted by nation-state actors and to develop security plans to mitigate those risks.

CTI can be used in a variety of ways, including:
• Threat hunting: Threat hunting is the process of searching for and identifying malicious activity on networks and systems. CTI can be used to help organizations identify known threats and to develop new threat detection rules.
• Incident response: CTI can be used to help organizations respond to cyberattacks by providing information about the threat actor, their tactics and techniques, and their potential goals.
• Risk assessment: CTI can be used to help organizations assess their risk of being impacted by cyber threats.
• Security planning: CTI can be used to help organizations develop and implement security controls to mitigate cyber threats.

CTI is an important data for organizations of all sizes to protect themselves from cyber threats. By using CTI, organizations can gain insights into the latest threats and take steps to protect themselves.

Here are some examples of how cyber threat intelligence can be used in practice:
• A security analyst at a financial services company uses CTI to identify a new phishing campaign targeting employees of financial services companies. The analyst is able to develop a new rule to detect the phishing emails and prevent employees from falling victim to the attack.
• A security incident response team at a healthcare organization uses CTI to identify a ransomware attack that has been launched against the organization. The team is able to use the CTI to identify the ransomware strain, the vulnerabilities that were exploited, and the attacker's IP addresses. The team is then able to take steps to mitigate the attack and recover from it.
• A risk management team at a government agency uses CTI to assess the risk of being targeted by a nation-state actor. The team is able to identify the nation-state actor's capabilities, their target industries, and their latest tactics and techniques. The team then uses this information to develop risk mitigation strategies.
• A security planning team at a retail company uses CTI to develop a security plan for the upcoming holiday season. The team is able to identify the known threats that are targeting retail companies during the holiday season. The team then uses this information to develop security controls to mitigate the threats.
